Crop Details

Explore detailed profiles for major crops — grains, fruits, vegetables, and cash crops with cultivation and nutritional data.

Category

Grain Crops

Rice (Oryza sativa)

Soil Type Clay / Loamy
Soil pH 5.5 – 6.5
Cultivation Transplanting / Direct Seeding
Harvest Time 110–150 days

Nutrition: Rich in carbohydrates, B vitamins, manganese, and iron.

Uses: Staple food, rice flour, animal feed, brewing, and biofuel production.

Wheat (Triticum aestivum)

Soil Type Loamy / Sandy Loam
Soil pH 6.0 – 7.0
Cultivation Direct Seeding (Rabi)
Harvest Time 100–120 days

Nutrition: High in protein, fiber, iron, zinc, and B-complex vitamins.

Uses: Flour for bread, pasta, cereals, animal feed, and starch production.

Corn (Zea mays)

Soil Type Well-drained Loam
Soil pH 5.8 – 7.0
Cultivation Direct Sowing
Harvest Time 65–105 days

Nutrition: Good source of fiber, vitamins A & B, magnesium, and antioxidants.

Uses: Direct consumption, corn oil, popcorn, starch, feed, and biofuel.

Category

Fruit Crops

Mango (Mangifera indica)

Soil Type Deep Alluvial / Loam
Soil pH 5.5 – 7.5
Cultivation Grafting / Budding
Harvest Time May – August

Nutrition: Excellent source of Vitamin C, A, folate, and potassium.

Uses: Fresh fruit, juices, pickles, chutneys, dried snacks, and jams.

Banana (Musa sp.)

Soil Type Rich Loamy
Soil pH 6.0 – 7.0
Cultivation Sucker / Tissue Culture
Harvest Time 9–12 months

Nutrition: Rich in potassium, Vitamin B6, magnesium, and natural sugars.

Uses: Fresh fruit, banana chips, smoothies, baking, and medicinal use.

Papaya (Carica papaya)

Soil Type Sandy Loam
Soil pH 6.0 – 6.5
Cultivation Seedling Transplanting
Harvest Time 10–12 months

Nutrition: High in Vitamin C, folate, Vitamin A, and digestive enzyme papain.

Uses: Fresh fruit, juices, medicinal extract (papain), green papaya salad.

Category

Vegetable Crops

Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum)

Soil Type Loamy / Sandy Loam
Soil pH 6.0 – 6.8
Cultivation Nursery Transplanting
Harvest Time 60–80 days

Nutrition: Rich in lycopene, Vitamins C and K, potassium, and folate.

Uses: Salads, sauces, ketchup, canning, juices, and culinary dishes.

Potato (Solanum tuberosum)

Soil Type Sandy Loam / Silt Loam
Soil pH 5.0 – 6.0
Cultivation Tuber / Seed Potato
Harvest Time 70–120 days

Nutrition: Good source of carbohydrates, Vitamin C, B6, potassium, and fiber.

Uses: Cooking, chips, flour, starch, alcohol production, and animal feed.

Cabbage (Brassica oleracea)

Soil Type Well-drained Loam
Soil pH 6.0 – 7.0
Cultivation Transplanting
Harvest Time 80–100 days

Nutrition: High in Vitamin K, C, folate, fiber, and glucosinolates.

Uses: Coleslaw, sauerkraut, stir-fry, soups, pickling, and fermentation.

Category

Cash Crops

Tea (Camellia sinensis)

Soil Type Deep Acidic Loam
Soil pH 4.5 – 5.5
Cultivation Vegetative Propagation
Harvest Time Perennial / 3–5 yr maturity

Nutrition: Contains antioxidants (catechins), caffeine, L-theanine, and polyphenols.

Uses: Green tea, black tea, white tea, oolong, medicinal extracts, and cosmetics.

Cotton (Gossypium sp.)

Soil Type Black Cotton / Deep Loam
Soil pH 5.8 – 8.0
Cultivation Direct Seeding
Harvest Time 150–180 days

Nutrition: Cottonseed oil is rich in Vitamin E and polyunsaturated fats.

Uses: Textile fiber, cottonseed oil, animal feed, medical bandages, and paper.

Sugarcane (Saccharum officinarum)

Soil Type Deep Loamy / Alluvial
Soil pH 6.0 – 7.5
Cultivation Stem Cuttings (Sett)
Harvest Time 12–18 months

Nutrition: Natural source of sucrose, iron, calcium, and B vitamins.

Uses: Sugar production, molasses, ethanol/biofuel, rum, and animal feed.
